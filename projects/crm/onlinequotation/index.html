From 14 Days to 1 Hour: Building a Quotation System That Scales

Reduced quote turnaround by 96%, unlocked $5M in revenue, and scaled quoting to 200+ installers without adding headcount.

What this project demonstrates
  • Strategic build-vs-buy decision making under revenue pressure
  • Designing systems for non-technical users at scale
  • Low-code + backend reuse to ship 10× faster
  • Operational automation as a growth lever, not just efficiency
  • Change management across 200+ service providers
Mobile-first, low-code quotation system optimized for speed, adoption, and parallel scale
The challenge

A rapidly growing Southeast Asian home services marketplace (3× YoY growth) was being slowed by a paper-based quotation process that took 7–14 days. Customers routinely booked with faster competitors, and 50% of complaints were tied directly to quote wait times.

The constraint: over 90% of 200+ installers had never used digital tools. Any solution had to work for skilled craftspeople—not just tech-savvy users.

The approach
1. Diagnose before building Applied Lean Six Sigma principles and interviewed 30+ installers to map the end-to-end quotation flow. The bottleneck wasn’t motivation—it was the absence of a system designed for real on-site conditions.
2. Make a deliberate build-vs-buy trade-off A custom build was estimated at six months—too slow under revenue pressure. I chose to adapt existing infrastructure: repurposed the internal pricing engine and layered a mobile-friendly, low-code interface on top. MVP shipped in 3 weeks.
3. Optimize for adoption, not elegance Delivered a Progressive Web App installers could pin to their home screen, embedded conditional pricing logic to eliminate manual errors, and added human-in-the-loop review before quotes were sent—balancing automation with trust.
Impact
<1 hr Quote turnaround
96% Faster lead time
$5M Revenue unlocked
0 Quote-related complaints

The system became the foundation for scaling installation services and later informed automation efforts across scheduling and payments.
